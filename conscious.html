Conscious is an app designed to reduce food waste by generating personalized recipes based on supply in fridge

My group and I created a prototype of an app called "Conscious" that addresses food waste management concerns.

It addresses this by...

  • Maintaining a list of perishable items within your fridge and relying information about their expiry
  • Generating personalized recipes based off user-inputed inventory/ingredients, prioritized by expiry date
  • Updating the grocery list based off current inventory and recipes
phase one: discovery
Initially, we went into this project with the intention to tackle the gap in technology and services that address food waste literacy and management solutions.

To investigate our claims...
  • We interviewed eight UofT students
  • We surveyed 77 students. We exported the survey data and used it aid in determining a need.
phase two: interpretation
In this phase, we summarized the data received in the Discovery stage, and using an affinity diagram, we sorted them into six common themes: awareness, emotions, cooking, wasting money, waste management, and food purchasing.

​ With this, we identified key themes and pain-points about how people handle food waste:
  • lack of grocery planning
  • lack of meal planning
  • and lack of education and awareness.
phase 3: ideation
In this stage, we first identified the needs of our targeted users that we plan to address.

These needs included...
  • A way to manage leftovers
  • A way to understand eating/disposal behaviors
  • Purchasing the right amount of ingredients
  • Education on food-expiry dates
​ With these needs in mind, we then generated 20 ideas as potential solutions- we wrote down whatever ideas we could think of, no matter how absurd.

We then determined which of these ideas had the the highest impact and feasibility. Those that did were centered around education when it comes to food waste management and portioning/planning meals.
phase four: experimention
In this phase, we created a working prototype of our idea using Adobe XD. We started off with a lo-fi prototype, demonstrated through a series of sketches, and received user feedback. With the criticism and praise in mind, we created our mid-fi prototype.
mid-fi prototype
lessons learned
We learned that people had already established mental concepts of how features should look or feel and they would be confused if something was designed differently.
